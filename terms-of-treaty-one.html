Promises Made from the Canadian Side

Many promises were made during the Treaty signing process from the Canadian side. These include:

  • Each band was to receive a reserve large enough to provide 160 acres for each family of five.
  • Each man, woman and child were to be given a gratuity (one time payment) of three dollars.
  • Each family were to be given a yearly annuity of $15 per a family of five
  • Government agreed to maintain a school on each reserve and to prohibit the introduction of selling liquor on each reserve

The Promises Made Outside the Treaty

Many promises were made outside of the Treaty-signing process. These include, but are not limited to:

  • A buggy for each chief
  • A buggy for the braves and councilors of each Chief
  • A bull and a cow for each Chief
  • A boar for each reserve
  • A sow for each chief, both a female and a male raised by farmers
  • A plough and harrow for each settler cultivating the ground
